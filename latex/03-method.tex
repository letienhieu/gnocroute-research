\section{Proposed Method: GNNocRoute-DRL}
\label{sec:method}
% ============================================================

This section details the GNNocRoute-DRL framework. We first establish the problem formulation and graph-theoretic motivation, followed by the GATv2 encoder design, the novel port-scoring decoupled architecture, and the fault-aware zero-shot training pipeline.

\subsection{Graph-Theoretic Motivation}
\label{sec:problem}

A NoC is naturally represented as a directed weighted graph $G = (V, E, X_v, X_e)$, where $V$ is the set of routers ($|V| = N$), $E$ is the set of physical links ($|E| = M$), $X_v \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times d_v}$ are node features, and $X_e \in \mathbb{R}^{M \times d_e}$ are edge features.

To capture both transient congestion and structural topology variations (including link failures), we expand the node features to a \textbf{12-dimensional fault-aware representation}: buffer occupancy per port (4), fault status per local link (4), VC utilization (1), historical congestion level (1), and relative physical coordinates (2).

The congestion imbalance (CI) is defined as the coefficient of variation ($\sigma/\mu$) of link utilization. Mesh $8\times8$ exhibits an extreme CI of 0.475, meaning nearly half of all links carry disproportionate loads. Central nodes possess betweenness centrality (BC) values $2-3\times$ higher than edge nodes, creating structural bottlenecks. This finding solidifies the core premise of our approach: a GNN that naturally captures macro-graph structures can proactively identify and bypass these inherent bottlenecks.

\subsection{Topology-Aware GNN Encoder}

We adopt GATv2 as the primary GNN encoder. The encoder processes the NoC graph state and produces dense topology-aware node embeddings $h_v \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$ for each router $v \in V$. The GATv2 encoder consists of two message-passing layers:

\begin{equation}
h_v^{(1)} = \sigma\left( \sum_{u \in \mathcal{N}(v)} \alpha_{vu}^{(1)} W^{(1)} x_u \right)
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
h_v^{(2)} = \sigma\left( \sum_{u \in \mathcal{N}(v)} \alpha_{vu}^{(2)} W^{(2)} h_u^{(1)} \right)
\end{equation}

where $\alpha_{vu}$ are the dynamic attention coefficients computed across the neighborhood $\mathcal{N}(v)$. Key hyperparameters include a hidden dimension of 64, 4 attention heads, edge features explicitly integrated via a projection matrix $W_e$, a dropout rate of 0.1, and ELU activations.

\subsection{Port-Scoring Decoupling Architecture}

The fundamental bottleneck in prior DRL-based NoC routing is the necessity of executing a neural network forward pass for every packet. GNNocRoute completely circumvents this via a decoupled port-scoring architecture.

During the offline training phase, the agent learns a continuous scoring function for each of the four physical directional ports $p \in \{N, S, E, W\}$. These learned policies are then projected and quantized into a \textbf{precomputed weight table}. For a Mesh $4\times4$, the complete mapping of all source-destination pairs to $4$-directional port scores fits into a highly compact \textbf{4 KB ROM weight table}.

At runtime (the fast timescale), the routing logic is stripped of all complex mathematics. When a packet arrives, the router simply executes a \textbf{single-cycle table lookup} to retrieve the precomputed scores for its minimal physical ports, selecting the direction with the highest value. This mechanism strictly adheres to Duato's protocol to guarantee deadlock freedom, consuming merely 0.13\% of the router's power budget and achieving an unprecedented 0.53 pJ/decision dynamic energy profile.

\subsection{Fault-Aware Training and Zero-Shot Transfer}

To achieve robustness against silicon wear-out and manufacturing defects, we introduce a \textbf{Fault-Aware Structural Dropout} mechanism during offline training. By artificially simulating up to 15\% random physical link failures during the GNN optimization phase, the encoder is forced to learn highly resilient, fault-tolerant pathing distributions rather than overfitting to a pristine deterministic grid.

Furthermore, because the GATv2 node embeddings capture generalized structural metrics (such as relative degrees and centrality) rather than absolute coordinates, the learned weights become inherently scale-invariant. To scale the routing policy from a $4\times4$ mesh to an $8\times8$ mesh without retraining, we apply a \textbf{Bilinear Weight Interpolation} mapping. This transforms the 4 KB weight matrix into a 16 KB matrix, enabling robust zero-shot generalization to larger network dimensions.

\subsection{Periodic Optimization Framework}

The architecture operates on a decoupled two-timescale paradigm:

\textbf{Slow Timescale (Background Execution):} The computationally heavy GATv2 inference (requiring approximately 5.5 ms on a standard CPU for a $4\times4$ mesh) executes entirely asynchronously. The weight tables are re-calculated and pushed to the routers only when macroscopic network conditions shift significantly (e.g., a physical link fails or a massive traffic regime change is detected).

\textbf{Fast Timescale (Per-Packet Routing):} Every individual packet is routed via the single-cycle hardware lookup table. By moving the neural network out of the critical path, GNNocRoute-DRL guarantees zero per-packet inference latency overhead while simultaneously maintaining the structural awareness of a deep graph model.
